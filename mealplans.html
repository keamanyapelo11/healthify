Healthy eating does not need to be expensive, boring, or difficult. The best meal plan is one you can afford and repeat consistently. Focus on balance: protein, carbohydrates, vegetables, fruit, and enough water.

Budget Breakfast Ideas

Oats Power Bowl

Oats cooked with water or milk, topped with banana and peanut butter.

Egg Toast Combo

Two eggs with whole wheat toast and tomato slices.

Yogurt Fruit Cup

Plain yogurt with chopped apple or berries.

Budget Lunch Ideas

Chicken Rice Bowl

Grilled chicken, rice, and mixed vegetables.

Tuna Sandwich

Whole wheat bread, tuna, lettuce, and cucumber.

Bean Plate

Beans, rice, and sautéed vegetables.

Dinner Ideas

Beef Stir Fry

Lean beef strips with vegetables and rice.

Baked Chicken Tray

Chicken pieces with potatoes and carrots.

Omelette Dinner

Three-egg omelette with vegetables and toast.

Healthy Snacks

  • Fruit
  • Boiled Eggs
  • Popcorn
  • Nuts (small portions)
  • Yoghurt

Smart Swaps

  • Sugary cereal → Oats
  • Chips → Popcorn
  • Soft drinks → Water
  • Fried food → Grilled food
  • Chocolate bar → Fruit and yogurt

Do not aim for a perfect diet. Aim for better choices most of the time.
